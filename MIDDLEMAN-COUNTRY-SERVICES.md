## The Core Principle

> MiddleMan does not copy-paste Uber into every country.
> MiddleMan maps how each country **actually moves** and builds services around that reality.

Every market has three layers:
1. **Transport Layer** — how people physically move (motos, taxis, buses, tuk-tuks, boda-bodas, jeepneys)
2. **Service Layer** — what people actually need (tours, cooks, babysitters, business promotion, errands)
3. **Culture Layer** — what makes this market unique (tourist vs local experience, regional vs city, language, currency)

## DIVISION: TWIKA — Chicago, USA 🇺🇸

**Transport Layer**
- Rideshare (car) — beats Uber/Lyft on price via reverse auction
- CTA-connected last mile (station pickup/dropoff)
- Cargo van / moving help
- Medical transport (non-emergency)

**Service Layer**
- Food delivery (beats DoorDash)
- Grocery delivery
- Laundry pickup/dropoff
- Handyman / home repair
- Childcare / babysitting
- Business promotion (flyer distribution, social media content creation, pop-up setup)
- Notary / document runner
- Airport pickup/dropoff

**Culture Layer**
- Chicago neighborhoods are hyperlocal — South Side, West Side, North Side have different economies
- Large Latino community (Mexican, Puerto Rican, Dominican) — Spanish language support
- Large Nigerian, Haitian, Jamaican diaspora — multilingual
- Currency: USD

## DIVISION: NEXO — Dominican Republic 🇩🇴

**Transport Layer**
- **Moto-concho** (motorcycle taxi) — the primary transport for locals, not tourists
- **Carro público** (shared taxi on fixed routes) — 4 passengers, fixed price per seat
- **Guagua** (minibus) — town-to-town, regional routes
- **Taxi** (private car) — for tourists and business travel
- **Bicicleta** (bicycle courier) — urban errands in Santo Domingo

**Service Layer — Tourist Experience (Authentic)**
- **Local Tour Guide** — NOT a tour bus. A person from the neighborhood who takes you to:
  - Local comedores (home-style restaurants, not tourist traps)
  - Bodegas and colmados (corner stores, social hubs)
  - Local nightclubs and colmados with music (merengue, bachata, dembow)
  - Hidden beaches locals actually use
  - Moto ride through the city like a local
- **Personal Cook** — hire a Dominican home cook for a meal, a day, or a week
- **Babysitter / Daycare** — huge need, especially for working parents and expats
- **Business Promotion** — DR has massive informal entrepreneur economy:
  - Social media content creation (reels, TikTok for small businesses)
  - Flyer/poster design and printing coordination
  - WhatsApp broadcast list management
  - Pop-up market setup and promotion
- **Errand Runner** — navigate the regional transport system on your behalf
- **Translation / Cultural Guide** — for expats and investors navigating DR bureaucracy
- **Property Caretaker** — many diaspora own property in DR, need local caretakers

**Regional Consideration**
- Santo Domingo (capital) — full service availability
- Santiago (second city) — full service availability
- Puerto Plata, Punta Cana, La Romana — tourist zones, higher demand for authentic local experience
- Barahona, Samaná, Jarabacoa — rural/eco zones, limited transport, specialized guides
- **Key insight:** A worker in Santiago cannot easily serve a job in Puerto Plata — regional routing is critical

**Culture Layer**
- Language: Spanish (primary), Haitian Creole (border regions)
- Currency: Dominican Peso (DOP) + USD accepted in tourist zones
- Time culture: flexible, relationship-based — workers are trusted through community reputation
- WhatsApp is the primary communication channel — not email, not app notifications

## DIVISION: PULSO — Puerto Rico 🇵🇷

**Transport Layer**
- **Carro público** (shared taxi, fixed routes between towns)
- **Uber** exists but expensive — MiddleMan undercuts via reverse auction
- **AMA bus** (limited, urban only)
- No metro — car-dependent island
- **Bicycle** in Old San Juan and Condado

**Service Layer**
- Rideshare (car) — direct Uber competitor
- Airport pickup/dropoff (SJU is a major hub)
- **Local Tour Guide** — Old San Juan, Bioluminescent Bay (Vieques/Fajardo), El Yunque rainforest, local food tours
- **Personal Cook** — mofongo, lechón, traditional PR cuisine for events
- **Event Setup / Teardown** — PR has massive party culture, quinceañeras, weddings
- **Babysitter / Childcare**
- **Business Promotion** — large small business and food truck economy
- **Hurricane Prep / Recovery** — seasonal demand for generators, tarps, cleanup crews
- **Medical Transport** — aging population, limited public transport

**Culture Layer**
- Language: Spanish (primary), English (official, widely spoken)
- Currency: USD
- Strong diaspora connection to NYC, Chicago — many families split between island and mainland

## DIVISION: NAIJA — Nigeria 🇳🇬

**Transport Layer**
- **Okada** (motorcycle taxi) — banned in Lagos but widespread elsewhere
- **Danfo** (yellow minibus, Lagos) — chaotic but essential
- **Keke NAPEP** (tricycle taxi) — cities and towns
- **BRT** (Bus Rapid Transit, Lagos) — limited routes
- **Bolt/Uber** exist but expensive — MiddleMan undercuts
- **Tricycle** (rural and semi-urban)

**Service Layer**
- Rideshare (car, keke, okada where legal)
- **Local Tour Guide** — Lagos street food scene, Lekki market, Oshodi, Nollywood studios, Benin City bronzes
- **Personal Cook** — jollof rice, egusi soup, suya, traditional Nigerian cuisine for events
- **Babysitter / Childcare** — massive need in urban centers
- **Business Promotion** — Nigeria has one of Africa's largest entrepreneur ecosystems:
  - Social media management (Instagram, TikTok for small businesses)
  - Content creation (Nollywood-style short videos)
  - Market stall promotion
- **Errand Runner / Personal Assistant** — navigate Lagos traffic on your behalf
- **Event Setup** — owambe (party) culture, massive demand

**Culture Layer**
- Language: English (official), Yoruba, Igbo, Hausa (regional)
- Currency: Nigerian Naira (NGN)
- 36 states — highly regional, Lagos ≠ Abuja ≠ Kano ≠ Port Harcourt
- Strong diaspora in UK, USA, Canada

**Transport Layer**

## The Database Architecture This Requires

Each market needs a `market_services` table:

```sql
CREATE TABLE market_services (
  id INT PRIMARY KEY AUTO_INCREMENT,
  market_id VARCHAR(20) NOT NULL,        -- 'chicago', 'dr', 'pr', 'jamaica', etc.
  service_key VARCHAR(50) NOT NULL,      -- 'rideshare', 'tour_guide', 'personal_cook', etc.
  service_name VARCHAR(100) NOT NULL,    -- Display name in local language
  service_name_es VARCHAR(100),          -- Spanish translation
  service_name_local VARCHAR(100),       -- Local language (Creole, Patois, etc.)
  base_price DECIMAL(8,2) NOT NULL,      -- Base price in local currency
  currency_code VARCHAR(5) NOT NULL,     -- 'USD', 'DOP', 'JMD', etc.
  transport_types JSON,                  -- ['moto', 'car', 'boat', 'bicycle']
  is_active BOOLEAN DEFAULT TRUE,
  is_tourist_facing BOOLEAN DEFAULT FALSE,
  description TEXT,
  icon VARCHAR(10)                       -- emoji icon
);
```
